Paper straw is never a solution
Nguyễn Quang Duy
@quangduy123
10/10/2019

As the plastic craze and its harmful effects take the media spotlight, many companies and people all across the globe are switching to paper straws instead of plastic as an eco-friendly alternative.
But does paper straw really solve the problem?
Making a paper straw requires growing a tree, cutting it down, and pulping and pressing it into a tube. Manufacturers then use fossil fuels to ship the straws to stores and cafés. Many paper straws on the market are not even compostable or recyclable, as promised. What can address the issue?
Replacing plastic with other materials, unlike most of us may think, does the environment no better. For each product being made it requires a considerable amount of natural resrouces and energy, thus contributing greatly to environmental pollution. Therefore, the root cause lies in eradicating consumerism, in reducing our consumption of goods.
Paper seems to be fine for the current days, but in a long term prospect, using paper straws will result in large consumption of wood for paper. So in terms of sustainable development, we can still search for many abundant source of materials that are suitable for the long duration. In Italy, there’s a new trend where people would use pasta as a replacement for plastic straws. Although we don’t have that much pasta in the ASEAN region, there are many other tube-shaped food that can be used to replace plastic like waffle rolls. Another change that can be made is about changing the design of handleable cups. We could easily change the lid shape , make it a little bit deeper or higher without the need to use any straws. Climate change - with the increase of heat - easily causing humans to be exposed with the toxics contained in the plastic that affects our health in tempature increases. So to move closer to the vision of the world without plastic, we have to do our best to remove plastic eventually from the aspects of our lives.
