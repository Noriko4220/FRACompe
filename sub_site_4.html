Indonesian Capital City: From Jakarta to Borneo
Nguyễn Quang Duy
@quangduy123
10/10/2019

According to latest updates, Indonesian government is spending $33 billion on its capital shift from Jakarta to the island of Borneo. This is by no means a surprise since the idea of relocating Indonesia's capital has been proposed for decades. It seems that President Joko Widodo may have been finally convinced by Jakarta's multitudinal environmental issues:
1. Jakarta is currently sinking by up to 6.7 inches per year, making it especially vulnerable to sea-level rise. For more than a decade, a steady stream of floods has devastated homes, vehicles, and businesses. Some of those floods have been deadly. In 2007, a flood killed around 80 people in Jakarta and inundated approximately 70,000 home.
2. The city also suffers from some of the world's worst air quality. According to the dynamic IQAirVisual index, Jakarta topped the charts for the world’s most polluted city at least half a dozen times this June. Tired of breathing in some of the world’s filthiest air, a group of activists and environmentalists in Jakarta has decided to sue the Indonesian government to take action.
However, Borneo is facing the same problems together with serious deforestation. Little has been done to improve the current condition in this future capital, and some environmentalists fear that arrival of new residents on the island could threaten the remaining healthy forests.
